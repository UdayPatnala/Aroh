The Product Requirements Document (PRD) defines the complete functional and non-functional requirements for the AROH ecosystem. It serves as the authoritative reference for product development and establishes what must be built, why it exists, who it serves, and how success will be measured. All implementation, design, testing, deployment, and documentation activities must align with this document.

AROH is a unified ecosystem consisting of multiple interconnected digital products that share a common platform foundation. Every product operates independently while integrating seamlessly through shared authentication, user profiles, membership, Aros economy, notifications, design system, SDK, CMS, and other platform services. The ecosystem must provide users with a consistent experience regardless of which product they access.

The primary objectives of AROH are to provide a scalable ecosystem architecture, eliminate duplicated functionality through reusable platform services, deliver premium user experiences, simplify long-term maintenance, support AI-assisted development, minimize operational costs, and enable future ecosystem expansion without major architectural redesign.

The primary stakeholders include end users, administrators, developers, future contributors, AI development assistants, and ecosystem product owners. Every stakeholder interacts with the platform through clearly defined responsibilities, permissions, and interfaces.

The primary user groups include visitors, registered users, premium members, administrators, operators, content managers, and future partner integrations. Access to functionality is determined exclusively through backend-managed roles and permissions.

The ecosystem must provide the following core capabilities:

Unified authentication and account management.

Centralized user profiles shared across all ecosystem products.

Role-based access control managed entirely by backend services.

Universal Aros wallet with immutable transaction history.

Membership management with support for multiple subscription levels.

Unified notification service supporting in-app and future communication channels.

Centralized CMS for managing dynamic ecosystem content.

Universal search across supported products and content.

Shared asset management for media and downloadable resources.

Standardized design system shared across every product.

Reusable SDK supporting common platform operations.

Event-driven communication between platform services.

Responsive user interfaces supporting desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

Accessibility-compliant interfaces following recognized accessibility standards.

Secure APIs exposing standardized contracts.

Scalable architecture supporting future ecosystem expansion.

The ecosystem homepage functions as the central hub that introduces the platform, highlights products, displays ecosystem announcements, promotes featured content, and provides entry points into individual products. Product pages remain focused exclusively on product-specific functionality while integrating shared platform capabilities where appropriate.

Administrative functionality is integrated directly into the primary application. Administrative tools become available only after backend authorization confirms appropriate privileges. Separate administrator authentication systems or dedicated administrative applications are intentionally avoided.

The CMS must support creation, editing, scheduling, previewing, publishing, archiving, restoring, and versioning of dynamic content. Supported content types include announcements, homepage sections, promotional banners, events, product highlights, informational cards, and future extensible content models.

The Aros economy operates exclusively through immutable transactions. Every balance modification generates an auditable transaction record. Direct balance manipulation is prohibited under all circumstances.

The platform must prioritize maintainability through reusable modules, centralized platform services, standardized development practices, documented interfaces, and consistent architectural principles. All new functionality must integrate through existing extension mechanisms whenever practical.

The platform must satisfy the following non-functional requirements:

High maintainability through modular architecture.

Horizontal scalability supporting future ecosystem growth.

Secure authentication, authorization, validation, and data protection.

Responsive performance across supported devices.

Accessibility compliance.

Reliable deployment and operational monitoring.

Comprehensive documentation synchronized with implementation.

Minimal operational cost during early development phases.

Future compatibility with planned Phase 2 and Phase 3 capabilities.

Success is achieved when the ecosystem delivers a unified, secure, scalable, maintainable, performant, and accessible platform capable of supporting multiple interconnected products while preserving architectural consistency, minimizing technical debt, and enabling efficient long-term development.
